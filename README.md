# SSUSI_Verif

SSUSI_Verif contains tools for comparing DMSP-SSUSI observations of the auroral oval to auroral oval models.

## Why is this useful?

Earth’s aurora is generated by energetic precipitation into our ionosphere at the polar regions. Auroras are observable in a region known as the auroral oval, which is affected by Earth’s magnetic field and its interaction with the solar wind. During major space weather events, solar wind-magnetosphere interaction can alter the auroral oval morphology and intensity, changing the energy deposition into the polar ionosphere. The auroral oval energy flux can be observed by DMSP-SSUSI, the Special Sensor Ultraviolet Spectrographic Imager aboard the Defense Meteorological Satellite Program. The instrument scans a partial swath of the auroral oval, which can then be compared with auroral models that are being developed not only to resolve limited observational capacities but also to achieve predictive capabilities. Auroral models can fill observational gaps, but their performance is not well quantified.

SSUSI_Verif is a toolkit to access case-study comparisons between auroral observations and model predictions as well as statistics over multiple storms. The toolkit tracks the geoeffectiveness of the storm event, calculates metrics to determine the differences between observed and modeled auroral oval response, and compares these metrics across a range of storms.
